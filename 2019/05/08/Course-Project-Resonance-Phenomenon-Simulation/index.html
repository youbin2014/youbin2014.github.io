Course Project: Resonance Phenomenon Simulation

Posted on 2019-05-08

Computational Physics Course Project

Coworker: Yu Ding, Mi Zhou

Advisor: John Wise

School of Physics, Georgia Tech


1 Abstract

Wave propagation is a classical mechanics problem which have been studied for years. Although its governing equation is simple, the outcome of the wave equation could present shocking complexity. In this paper we attempt to solve a practical question of how the water wavetravels in a particular structure. Here we show that the excited water along the edge of our system is in fact not created by wave super-positioning. This agrees with previous result obtained via the method of molecular dynamics[1]. We consider our results to be a useful subsidy for the previous results, working as a more “analytical” explanation for ruling out origins of water excitations. We hope this result could be extended into more general problems of surface waves for Newtonian fluid.
