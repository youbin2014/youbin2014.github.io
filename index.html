Research Internship:Improving Performance of CNN algorithm ( YOLO )

Posted on 2019-05-09 | In Projects

Advisor: Dr.Jinjun Wang

This is a research internship in the Artificial Intelligence and Robotics Institute in Xi’an Jiaotong University. Under the supervision of Prof. Jinjun Wang, I worked in improving the performance of YOLO algorithm by employing seperable convolution to the CNN.

We held the point that seperable convolution could reduce the required parameters in CNN. In this case, it would speed up the training. The largest difficulty is to rewrite the GPU part of YOLO codes. We had finished the CPU part of YOLO codes. To rewrite the GPU codes, I learnt CUDA programming.

Unfortunately, we didn’t get a good result in the end. However, I learnt a lot about CNN algorithm and knew how YOLO works in the mathematic and code level. I am grateful for the guidance of Prof. Jinjun Wang and Dr. Wenli Huang.

Resonance Phenomenon Simulation

Posted on 2019-05-08 | In Projects

Advisor: Dr. John Wise
Coworker: Yu Ding, Mi Zhou

1 Abstract

Wave propagation is a classical mechanics problem which has been studied for years. Although its governing equation is simple, the outcome of the wave equation could present shocking complexity. In this paper we attempt to solve a practical question of how the water wavetravels in a particular structure. Here we show that the excited water along the edge of our system is in fact not created by wave super-positioning. This agrees with previous results obtained via the method of molecular dynamics[1]. We consider our results to be a useful subsidy for the previous results, working as a more “analytical” explanation for ruling out origins of water excitations. We hope this result could be extended into more general problems of surface waves for Newtonian fluid.

Molecular Dynamics Simulation and Visualization

Posted on 2019-05-07 | In Projects

Advisor: Dr. Fanglin Peng

3D-Visualization of Molecules’ Distribution

In this project, a simple molecular dynamics simulation of isolated systems was conducted. We simulated the microscopic process in which the nitrogen molecules diffuses from the center to the surrounding until the equilibrium state is reached in a certain volume of adiabatic square box. In this process, we computed and visualized the velocity distribution, pressure, average kinetic energy and temperature of nitrogen until it reached the equilibrium state. For the sake of simple computation, the parameters involved in the process are dimensionless. The intermolecular Lennard-Jones potential energy and the complete elastic collision of the box wall are considered in this mechanical model. Also, The Leap-Frog algorithm is used to compute the molecular motion. The dynamic results were visualized in real-time.

Locating a Stick on Earch according to Sun Shadow

Posted on 2019-05-06 | In Projects

Coworker: Yi Biao, Hanyang Liu
This is a question in the China Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling(CUMCM).

Question: Location according to Sun Shadow

How to determine where and when the video was taken is an important aspect of video data analysis. The sun shadow localization technique is a method of determining the location and date of a video by analyzing the variation in the sun-shadow of an object in the video.

The problem is simplified as: according to the coordinate data of the sun-shadow vertices of a fixed vertical rod on the horizontal ground, establishing a mathematical model to determine the location and date of the rod.
